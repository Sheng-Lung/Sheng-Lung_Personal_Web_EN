Introduction

Hello! I'm Sheng-Lung, Lin (林 聖龍) — a geophysicist passionate about seismic signal analysis and earthquake precursors, hoping to design effective early warning systems so that one day, people may live free from the fear of earthquakes and in a safer world.

🧑‍💼 About Me

  • Reflecting on the past

    I earned my master’s degree from National Taiwan University, where I specialized in multi-channel seismic reflection surveys. My thesis, “Pseudo-3D Seismic Data Processing and Application in the Southern Okinawa Trough,” involved constructing pseudo-3D seismic images using 2D seismic reflection data to study crustal structures in the region. Following that, I pursued doctoral research at the University of Iceland, where I focused on: extracting velocity-based seismic signals, automatically identifying strong ground motion using machine learning.

  • Currently

    My research interests revolve around Geophysics, seismic wave analysis, and deep learning models. I am especially interested in detecting earthquake precursors and potential early warning signals in pre-mainshock seismic waveform data.


    Future Plan

    I am currently actively seeking Ph.D. opportunities, with the hope of successfully completing a doctoral degree and contributing to the advancement of earthquake early warning systems.
